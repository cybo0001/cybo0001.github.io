About

Matt is a freelance game developer from the heart of Canada. When he was a lot shorter, he made little games in RPG Maker, Neverwinter Nights, and good old pen and paper for his own amusement. Nowadays, he works with friends, small professional teams or individually to create games across multiple platforms. Doing this kind of work, especially on your own, means he's had to wear a lot of hats - and the skills picked up along the way include design, direction, programming, basic illustration, modelling, animation, writing, team management, and publishing.

Toll Trouble (2021)

Ever wanted to be a toll booth operator? Well, now you can! Deal with the annoyance of everyone giving you fistfuls of inexact change, harass people who try to skip the barrier, and make sure everyone's registration is up to par! (Not yet complete)

Bloo (2021)

A physics free-drawing puzzle game that works on PC and mobile. First game I ever finished and published online! Draw lines to help the little blue ball get to his goal.

Goal: Implement a hand drawing physics mechanic and turn it into a puzzle game.

Insights Gained: The last 10 percent is 90 percent of the work. Game feel is everything. Cutting back on mechanics that don't impact the core gameplay loop is key. Showcasing and live testing with actual people can show unexpected results that end up in a lot of extra work, so do it early and do it fast.

Humanz

A funny little idle game I'm working on with some friends. It's a colony sim where you manage the lives and growth of crash-landed, strange little humans that land on even stranger planets.

Fix My Car: Mad Road

The second commercial game I worked on for FireRabbit Inc. It's a point and click object finding game where you fix up a car in a post-apocalyptic world to impress a girl, really. It was the company's biggest (in terms of size at least) game shipped. I was responsible for level design, writing, and promotional materials. While working on this I also got to build some handy editor tools to really speed things along.

Banzai's Buffet (2015)

My first real playable project. It's a restaurant sim that involves a monkey. You can't really do any restaurant management stuff, but you can sure mess around with a hard day's labor of cooking, cleaning, and servicing guests. You need a 360 controller plugged in to play! If you can make more than 1,500 banzaibucks in a day on hard mode, I salute you!

Kazoo Kars (2014)

The first prototype I ever made using Unity3D. You play as a car with really janky physics and try to get through an obstacle course and reverse park while keeping at least 1 capsule in your trailer. Really difficult, too.
